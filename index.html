TOPIC: STEP-TO-STEP GUIDE ON HOW TO BUILD A HIGH-VALUE-MINDED COMMUNITY IN NIGERIA

Introduction

A high-value-minded community is one where people are focused on building relationships and finding common ground, rather than just focusing on their own individual interests. It can also be said that a high high-value-minded community is where people value cooperation and collaboration over competition. A high-value-minded community can also be said as one that addresses inequality, builds trust, invests in education and creates spaces for dialogue. When people feel like they belong to a community, it can really help to create a sense of shared purpose which can lead to building of a community, even a nation.
Building a high-value-minded community is a vital necessity to the growth and development of any vibrant community/society/nation. No man is an Island, we all belong to a community directly or indirectly, hence, there is need to actively engage in building a high-value-minded communities in Nigeria.

Concept of a community

A community is a group of people with diverse cultural beliefs and norms but share the same core values, living together in a particular geographical area or location. It can also be defined as a group of people with different backgrounds, but share common values and live in the same area.

Concept of values

Values are basically the principles that guide our decisions and behaviour. Thay can be things like honesty, integrity, respect, fairness, kindness, and so on. Some values are universal while others might be spec ific to certain cultures or religions. But overall, values are what guide us to make choices that reflect our priorities and beliefs.
Values play important roles in building high-value-minded communities in a country because good values breed credibility, diligence, patriotism, tolerance, orderliness, etc.

Sources of values

Like I said earlier, values reflect our sense of right and wrong. They help us create the future we want.The decisions we make everyday are a reflection of our values. Almost all of our values are mostly learn from our parents and extended families. Our family values stem from our social and cultural values. Sometimes, new life experiences may change values we previously held.

  • Individual values: These are the personal beliefs and principles that guide an individual's actions. They also reflect how we live our life and what we consider important for our own self-interests. Individual values include enthusiasm, creativity, humility, personal fulfilment and innovation.

  • Relationship values: These reflect how we relate to other people in our life, such as friends, family, bosses, colleagues, etc. Theseb include openness, trust, generosity and caring.

  • Social values: Are values that reflect how we relate to society. Social values include justice, freedom, respect and responsibilities.

Guide on how to build a high-value-minded communities in Nigeria

  1. Good Leadership: Strong leadership is absolutely essential to creating a high-value-minded community. A leader can set the tone for the community, inspire people to work together, and help to resolve conflict. Without strong leadership, a community can easily fall into division and disarray. A leader needs to have a strong moral compass in order to lead a community towards a high-value-minded future. A leader needs to exhibits the following characteristics:
    • Commitment: Without a commitment to ethical principles, a leader could easily abuse their power or lose the trust of the community.

    • Charismatic: A charismatic leader can inspire people and make them feel like they're part of something bigger than themselves.

    • Authenticity: Authenticity is definitely important for a leader. People need to feel like they can trust a leader and that the leader is being genuine. A leader who isn't authentic can quickly lose the trust of the community.

    • Humility: A leader who is humble can admit mistakes, learn from others, and stay grounded in the face of success. On the other hand, a leader who is arrogant or self-centered can alienate people and make poor decisions.


  2. Diversity: Diversity is referred to a variety of things, like race, gender, age, or socioeconomic status. A diverse community is one that has people from a variety of backgrounds and perspectives. Diversity can bring new ideas, different viewpoints, and fresh perspectives to a community.

  3. Empathy: Empathy is defined as understanding and sharing the feelings of another. People need to understand who others are and accept who they are. Focusing on how we can grow together should be our ultimate goal.

  4. Respect: Mutual respect makes us human because that's what we all need. We should respect everyone around us those we meet, not minding the differences in our backgrounds. Our communities need this to function well.

  5. Love: Love is a powerful emotion that builds bonds and encourages positivity. Having love in our hearts keeps us from feeling the need to harm others. Love helps us acknowledge the similarities we all share rather than the differences of color or religion.

  6. Loyalty: Loyalty is a value that binds us to a person, thing or sentiment. With loyalty, we do not betray. If we all shared loyalty, it would help us build the strength needed to stand up against something that would harm our society.

  7. Trust: Another important aspect of a high-value-minded community is that it's built on trust. People need to feel like they can rely on each other and that they're all working towards the same goals. Without trust, it's hard to build the kind of cooperation that's needed to address big challenges.

  8. Honesty: One form of honesty in society is accepting yourself. With honesty, you can admit your flaws and take the necessary steps to improve yourself. When we can admit to our flaws it can help someone else admit theirs. Ultimately, we can all help each other become better people.

  9. Sense of purpose: Another key element of a high-value-minded community is a shared sense of purpose. People need to feel like they're all working towards something bigger than themselves, something that's meaningful and important. It could be something like improving the quality of life in the community, or creating opportunities for future generations. Without that shared sense of purpose, it's hard to maintain the motivation needed to cooperate and work together.

  10. A sense of shared identity: A sense of shared identity is all about creating a feeling of belonging and connection within a community. It can be based on things like shared values, history, language, or culture. When people feel like they're part of a shared identity, it can give them a strong sense of connection and purpose. It can help to create a feeling of "we're all in this together".
